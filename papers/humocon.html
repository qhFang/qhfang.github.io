Abstract

This study delves into the realm of concept discovery for human motion understanding by leveraging advanced machine learning techniques. Diverging from previous approaches that focus on low-level motion features, we argue that understanding human behavior necessitates discovering high-level semantic concepts that bridge the gap between raw motion data and human-interpretable meanings. In light of this, we present HuMoCon, a novel framework for concept discovery in human motion understanding that effectively captures nuanced movement patterns and their semantic relationships. Our approach demonstrates significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods through comprehensive experiments on multiple benchmarks, showcasing the effectiveness of our concept-driven approach for human motion analysis and understanding.

Overview

HuMoCon introduces a novel approach to human motion understanding through automated concept discovery. Our framework identifies meaningful motion concepts and their relationships, enabling more interpretable and effective human behavior analysis.

Method

Our method consists of three main components: (1) Motion Encoder that processes raw motion sequences, (2) Concept Discovery Module that identifies semantic concepts from motion patterns, and (3) Concept Reasoning Module that establishes relationships between discovered concepts for comprehensive understanding.
